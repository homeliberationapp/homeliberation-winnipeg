Why Selling As-Is Makes Sense

The math rarely works out for you to do repairs:

  • You pay retail for repairs (contractors charge 30-50% more than investor rates)
  • You pay interest on loans or credit cards to fund repairs
  • You waste months finding contractors, managing work, dealing with delays
  • You pay carrying costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities) during repairs
  • You assume risk that repairs uncover more problems or cost more than estimated
  • You still pay realtor fees (5-6%) after all that

Example: A house worth $300K needs $60K in repairs

  • Repair costs: $60,000
  • Carrying costs (6 months): $12,000
  • Realtor fees (5%): $15,000
  • Total cost to sell: $87,000
  • Net to you: $213,000

Our offer might be $220,000 cash, no repairs, close in 10 days.

You net MORE by selling to us, even though our offer is lower than retail. Plus you avoid 6 months of stress, contractor headaches, and financial risk.

How We Calculate Our Offer

We use the wholesale formula (and we're upfront about it):

  • After Repair Value (ARV): What the house is worth fully renovated
  • Minus Repair Costs: Our actual contractor costs (typically 40-60% of retail estimates)
  • Minus Our Profit: $20K-$40K depending on deal size and risk
  • Minus Holding Costs: Mortgage, taxes, insurance while we renovate
  • Minus Closing Costs: Legal fees, title insurance, realtor fees when we resell
  • = Maximum Offer Price

All contracts are structured to protect us and minimize our risk. We're running a business, not a charity. But we're honest, transparent, and we close fast with cash.
